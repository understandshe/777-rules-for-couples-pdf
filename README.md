### The Parallel Pizza Phase
It was a Tuesday. I remember bc Tuesdays are trash days and also the day my husband forgot we were married.

Not forgot forgot. Just... stopped seeing me. I was the woman who loaded the dishwasher. Who reminded him about his mom's birthday. Who sat on the other side of the couch scrolling while he watched videos about fish tanks. For three hours. On a Tuesday.

We hadnt fought. No big drama. Just this slow fade into roommates who shared a bathroom and occasionally ordered pizza.

The pizza was the breaking point. I wanted pepperoni. He wanted veggie. We got separate pizzas. Ate them in separate rooms. And I sat there with my greasy box thinking - this is it. This is how marriages die. Not with cheating or screaming. With parallel pizza consumption.

### What 777 Actually Means When You're Drowning
Look, the **777 rules for marriage** sound fancy. But its just math for people who forgot how to love on purpose.

**Every 7 Days: A Date.** Not "we're both home watching Netflix." Actual pants with buttons, out of the house, looking at each other's faces date. Even if you're broke. Even if you're tired. Even if youd rather die than put on real pants.

**Every 7 Weeks: A Night Away.** We couldnt afford a hotel at first. So we swapped houses with my sister. Sent the kids there, stayed in her empty apartment with her cat who hates us. Best night of that year. Bc we were somewhere that didnt have our life smeared all over it.

**Every 7 Months: The Big One. Vacation.** We saved for a year for our first one. Drove six hours to a cabin with no wifi. Fought about directions for two of those hours. Then sat on a porch watching fireflies and remembered why we picked each other in the first place.

### The Thing Nobody Tells You About Rules
Some people say this kills spontaneity. That love should be natural. Effortless. Those people arent married to my husband. Or maybe anyone.

Love IS work. Not hard work. But intentional work. Like watering a plant. You dont wait until its dying and then flood it. You do a little bit, consistently, bc you want it to live.

The **777 rules for dating** - or marriage, or whatever you're in - they're just a watering schedule. Bc left alone, we forget. The world fills up the space. Kids, jobs, the endless scroll of nothing that eats your evening.

### When the Rule Isnt Enough
I gotta say this part. Bc I read about that actor - Andrew something? Who cheated even with the rule? His wife gave him the **777 rules pdf** like a contract. Like "do this or else."

Rules dont fix broken. They maintain what's still alive. If someone already checked out, already betrayed, already decided you're not enough - then no schedule, no vacation, no magic number fixes that. We were lucky. We caught it early. The pizza was a warning, not an ending.

### The Real 777 Rule Presentation
I made my husband a slideshow once. Yes, really. Powerpoint and everything. "777 Rules: Why We Need This Or We'll Die." Very romantic. He laughed for ten minutes. Then he listened.

Bc here's what I learned: men dont always know how to say "I'm scared we're losing this." They just retreat. Into work, into hobbies, into fish tank videos. Not bc they dont care. Bc they dont know how to care out loud.

The rule gives them language. "Its been seven weeks, we need our night." Not "we need to talk" - which every man hears as "you failed." Just... a plan. A thing to do. An action to take.
